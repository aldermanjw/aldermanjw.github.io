History of Board Games

According to Peter Attia, at

https://medium.com/swlh/the-full-history-of-board-games-5e622811ce89
Most people don’t realize board games are actually pre-historic, meaning we had board games before we had written language. So, what was the very first game?.. Dice! A piece that’s essential in most board games today was the basis of humanity’s oldest games. A series of 49 small carved painted stones were found at the 5,000-year-old Başur Höyük burial mound in southeast Turkey. These are the earliest gaming pieces ever found. Similar pieces have been found in Syria and Iraq and seem to point to board games originating in the Fertile Crescent. The Crescent is comprised of regions around the Nile, Tigris, and Euphrates River in the Middle East. The same region that invented booze, papyrus, breath mints, and calendars, which are also required when planning your game night!

  • About Each Type
Abstract strategy board games
Tend to be theme less focus entirety on strategy than luck, promote one player overtaking the other. This game is great for people who like to think.
Card games
Have multiple player each player, card game focus on both strategy and luck, the point of card games is to win the game. each player as a equal chance to win. Card games are great to play with friend and family.
Puzzle and mystery games
is a game where everyone work together to solve the mystery or problem they are faced. This type of game is great for build teamwork skill since everyone is working together to solve the mystery. Also for the sore loser who had difficulty with losing.
